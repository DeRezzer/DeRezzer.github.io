The Obelisk

The angular mass that jutted from the root of creation arched upward and pierced into the sky. There

was no sound and there was no movement. Time slipped by slowly as if halted by a force pushing

against it. Argaven stood, transfixed upon the blue obelisk. A wave of unknown emotion swelled within

him and tears rolled down his face. He had never seen anything such as this mass before. A holy wonder

that belonged to him and him alone.

Argaven studied the blue surface of the mass and found that it was smooth. And in that smoothness, he

could see the reflection of the elements that made physical, the world around him. Time had shape, and

that shape was made of an endlessly complex weave of silver strings spun around an elegant crystal. The

very air around him was a strange color. It was a dull grey, as if inked by a spill of black. There was sound

that existed only within the blue reflection. The sound was an endless drone of harmonic overtones that

were crisp and sweet. But in that sweetness, could be heard the end of the world. A shattering of earth

and a collision of stars. The sudden creation of the universe and It’s silence all together. There was

tragedy in its design but there was a mark of beauty as well. Argaven stared into the crystal surface of

the obelisk. And within its complex, holy, terrible intent, he surmised he was peering into the very

depths of oblivion.Try as he might, Argaven could not look away from the mystic reflection within the holy artifact. There

was death and creation and the distant cries of beings, eons long gone. He could not close his eyes. In

time they dried and withered away but his sight did not falter. He could see the shape of time stretch,

contract, and fold. And within the creases he could read humanity’s fate as if it were a map. And though

they were his eyes and they were good, he then saw beyond the crisp horizon of mortality and accepted

this gift of doom. Argaven felt his skin harden and corrode away as the years slipped through the fine

cracks of space and pressure of gravity. A god is not born from sweetness and warmth. A god is born

from the crushing weight of the universe falling upon a single man
